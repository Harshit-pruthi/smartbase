Chapter 4: Dissolution of Partnership Firm

Estimated: 4 hours 12 Topics 25% Completed

Introduction

Dissolution of a partnership firm means that the firm closes its business and comes to an end. In this process, the assets of the firm are sold and liabilities are paid off. The accounting treatment for dissolution involves closing the books of accounts and settling the amounts due to partners.

Key Concepts

  • Difference between dissolution of partnership and dissolution of firm
  • Settlement of accounts
  • Realization of assets and payment of liabilities
  • Preparation of realization account
  • Treatment of goodwill
  • Distribution of accumulated profits and losses

Accounting Treatment

The process of dissolution involves:

  1. Preparation of Realization Account
  2. Closing the books of accounts
  3. Settlement of partners' loans
  4. Payment of partners' capital accounts
  5. Distribution of remaining cash

Realization Account

Realization Account is prepared to ascertain the profit or loss on the realization of assets and payment of liabilities. It is a nominal account that records:

  • All assets (except cash) transferred at book value
  • All liabilities (except partners' loans) transferred at book value
  • Sale of assets
  • Payment of liabilities
  • Realization expenses
