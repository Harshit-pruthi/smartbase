Overview And Definitions

Dissolution of Partnership Firm

Dissolution of a partnership firm refers to the process of ending the relationship between the partners of the firm. This involves discontinuing the business, settling accounts, and realizing assets and liabilities.

Key Definitions

  • Dissolution: The process of ending the existence of the partnership relationship among all partners.
  • Realization: The process of converting partnership assets into cash.
  • Insolvency: When a partner is unable to pay their share of losses or liabilities.
  • Goodwill: The value of the firm's reputation and customer relationships.

Modes of Dissolution

There are several ways a partnership firm can be dissolved:

  1. Dissolution by Agreement: When all partners agree to dissolve the firm.
  2. Compulsory Dissolution: When the business becomes illegal or all partners except one become insolvent.
  3. Dissolution on the Happening of Certain Events: Such as the expiration of the fixed term, death of a partner, or completion of the specific venture.
  4. Dissolution by Notice: In partnership at will, any partner can dissolve by giving notice.
  5. Dissolution by Court: When a court orders dissolution due to insanity, misconduct, or persistent breach of agreement.

Accounting Process

The accounting process for dissolution involves:

  1. Closing the books of accounts
  2. Preparing a realization account
  3. Settling assets and liabilities
  4. Distributing any remaining cash to partners
